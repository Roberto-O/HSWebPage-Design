Volleyball is a game for two teams, usually of six players, in which a large ball is hit by hand over high net, the aim being to score points by making the ball reach the ground on the oppononent's side of the court.
The game of volleyball called "mintonette," was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan after the invention of basketball only four years before. Morgan, a graduate of the Springfield college of the YMCA, designed the game to be a combination of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball.
