Okinawa Churaumi Aqarium

 

Okinawa's wealth of nature, history and culture can be experienced at Ocean Expo Park, a place where you can even play with dolphins.
Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium is the second largest aquarium in the world and one of many popular attractions at Ocean Expo Park.

At the Aquarium, an intriguing world of marine life found in Okinawa is spread out before you. A sea of coral dazzled with sunlight exhibits living coral colonies in large scale.
With its enormous capacity, a gigantic tank called The Kuroshio Sea Tank the largest fish in the world, the whale shark, and the world's first successfully bred-in-captivity manta can also be seen. Moreover, you can experience the sea of Okinawa when you journey into the deep sea attraction, which recreates Okinawa's mystery-enshrouded deep sea.

A kaleidoscopic variety of fish swim in a large tank, including the 8.5 meter whale shark and a manta ray with the world's record for longevity in captivity. The large acrylic window through which this impressive scene is displayed measures 8.2m in height, 22.5m in width, and is 60cm thick. We are confident that you will be impressed and excited to be within hands' reach of the sharks, rays, and other wide-ranging fish that thrive in the Kuroshio Sea.

We hope to see your student come with us to experience this adventure!
